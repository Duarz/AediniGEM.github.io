Method

Our plan is to create a transgenic mosquito that can spread a gene drive through a local population. A gene drive is an inserted genetic element inside the animal's own DNA that confers certain changes. Part of those changes is that the drive copies itself onto both alleles, so that it becomes impossible not to be passed onto the next generation.

"X-shredder"

In our case we are going to be working with an X-shredder type of gene drive. What it does is it skews the male to female ratio towards the entire population eventually being male. The gene drive does this by inactivating male mosquito's X-chrmosome, so that only Y chromosomes can be given to a zygote, making all progeny male. That generation then does the same to its progeny untill the population dies off from lack of mating parters. The method has been confirmed to work through the experiments of Windbichler et al. in 2007 and 2012.

Modelling

Models made so far by Deredec et al. in 2011 show that the method can be successful on a massive scale. If a genetically modified male population equal to 1% of the native population is released the entire local group will be wiped out within a year. We have improved upon the assumptions in the paper by utilizing a CRISPR based gene drive which is overall more efficient and stable than a zinc-finger based one.



Safety

Safety is important to consider. We’ve identified a reversible gene drive from the Wyss Institute which can soften the potential ecological blow of driving down mosquito populations. Regulations from the Health and Safety Executive agency state that we would require a Class 4 lab and £1,225 for the associated license when working with GMOs. Other general administrative tasks include the following: equipment use instruction, disinfection procedures and waste handling, storage, spillage & accident reporting procedures, transport packaging, and risk and COSHH assessments.
